  • By comparing results in Q2 and Q3 we can get:

\begin{aligned} \langle x\rangle &= 0
\langle x^2\rangle &\propto \frac{1}{\lambda} \cdot t \end{aligned}

a reasonable guess would be: \(\langle x^2\rangle = \frac{t}{\lambda}\)

3.2 Analytical solution

By Elenaâ€™s hint, the probability mass function for position \(x\) at certain time \(t\) is:

\(P(x\mid t) = \sum_{m=0}^{\infty} \frac{m!}{\left(\frac{m-x}{2}\right)!\left(\frac{m+x}{2}\right)!} \left(\frac{1}{2}\right)^m \left(\frac{t}{\lambda}\right)^m \frac{e^{-t/\lambda}}{m!}.\)

3.2.1 Mean

Considering the symmetry of this distribution: \begin{aligned} P(x=i\mid t) &= \sum_{m=0}^{\infty} \frac{m!}{\left(\frac{m-x}{2}\right)!\left(\frac{m+x}{2}\right)!} \left(\frac{1}{2}\right)^m \left(\frac{t}{\lambda}\right)^m \frac{e^{-t/\lambda}}{m!}.
&= P(x=-i \mid t) \end{aligned} Therefore: \begin{aligned} \langle x\rangle &= \sum_x x\cdot P(x\mid t)
&= \sum_{i=1}^{m} i \cdot P(x=i\mid t) -i \cdot P(x=-i\mid t)
&= 0 \end{aligned}

3.2.2 Variance

Because of the mean of this distribution is 0:

\begin{aligned} var(x) = \langle x^2\rangle &= \sum_x x^2\cdot P(x\mid t)
&= \sum_{m=0}^{\infty} (x^2 \frac{m!}{\left(\frac{m-x}{2}\right)!\left(\frac{m+x}{2}\right)!} \left(\frac{1}{2}\right)^m) \cdot \left(\frac{t}{\lambda}\right)^m \frac{e^{-t/\lambda}}{m!}.
&= \sum_{m=0}^{\infty} m \cdot \left(\frac{t}{\lambda}\right)^m \frac{e^{-t/\lambda}}{m!}.
&= \frac{t}{\lambda} \sum_{m=0}^{\infty} \left(\frac{t}{\lambda}\right)^m \frac{e^{-t/\lambda}}{m!}.
&= \frac{t}{\lambda} \end{aligned}
